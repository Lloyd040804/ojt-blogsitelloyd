Journal Summary

My On-the-Job Training (OJT) at TESDA was a meaningful experience that allowed me to apply my academic knowledge in real workplace settings. I was mainly assigned in handling CACs documents where I was responsible for organizing, filing, and assisting in the processing of important records. This task helped me develop a sense of accuracy, patience, and responsibility.

Aside from document-related tasks, I was also able to observe and support office operations, communicate with staff, and assist in day-to-day activities. These experiences improved my communication skills, teamwork, and adaptability in a professional environment.

Overall, the OJT allowed me to grow both personally and professionally. I learned the importance of discipline, efficiency, and collaboration. Most importantly, it boosted my confidence in preparing for my future career in the IT field and gave me a better understanding of what it is like to be part of a professional workforce.
